# Contact Prefix Routing Fixes

- Contact id prefix ownership is centralized in shared helpers. Managers and
  connector result merging must only pass a connector the contact ids that belong
  to its family, for example `telegram@...` for Telegram and `google@...` for
  Gmail or Google Calendar.
- Connector-native list and search results are normalized, filtered by the owning
  connector family, and then merged with locally inferred candidates. Foreign ids
  returned by a connector are ignored instead of leaking across subscriptions.
- Contact context requests are split per connection. A connector is skipped when a
  requested context id set contains no ids owned by that connector.
- The command connector contacts parser accepts terminal contacts frames after
  progress or log lines in JSONL-style stdout and scans from the end for the last
  contacts/contact-context frame.
- Telegram contact ids require stable handles. Numeric-only Telegram fallbacks and
  bot-originated chat or sender payloads are not valid contact identities.
